HA LONG BAY

A UNESCO World Heritage Treasure

Ha Long Bay as known as a UNESCO World Heritage Site is famous because its stunning limestone mountains raising up from emerald waters that formed from thousand years ago. You also can have a chance to explore amazing caves as well as the culture of Ha Long.

Where Myth Meets Reality

Legend has it that Ha Long Bay's limestone formations were created when dragons spat out jewels and jade into the bay to create a natural fortress against invaders. The jewels turned into islands and islets, forming the magnificent landscape we see today.

Ha Long Bay's Tranquil Beauty

Comprising around 1,600 limestone islets and towering karst formations, Ha Long Bay is a geological marvel that took millions of years to form. The bay's emerald-green waters and lush vegetation atop the islets make it a truly otherworldly destination.

A Symphony of Diversity

Ha Long Bay is not just about rock formations and water; it's also home to a diverse ecosystem with over 200 species of fish and 450 different kinds of mollusks. The bay is also inhabited by several floating fishing villages where people have lived for generations.
